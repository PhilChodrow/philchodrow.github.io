Welcome!

I’m Dr. Phil Chodrow, an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science at Middlebury College. My pronouns are he/him/his.

Research

In my research, I study mathematical and computational techniques for understanding our increasingly connected and complex world. A lot of what I do falls under the heading of network science. I’ve recently worked on spectral algorithms for analyzing hypergraphs, opinion dynamics on graphs, and dominance hierarchies in human and animal societies. I also work on data science projects to promote equity and sustainability.

Teaching

In Fall 2022, I am teaching CSCI 0145: Introduction to Computing.

I am a passionate educator, enthusiastic about evidence-based pedagogy, student-centered design, and inclusion in the classroom. Topics I teach include introductory computing, network science, and machine learning. Read more about my mission and the courses I teach.
